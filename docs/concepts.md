# What makes a good theme

A theme is a curated look for one widget. The catalog is small on purpose —
the goal is a handful of looks each streamer would actually be glad to find,
not a wall of near-duplicates.

## A good theme is intentional

It looks like someone made a decision about every value. Colors relate to
each other. The font fits the mood. Nothing is left at a default because the
author didn't get to it.

## A good theme reads at distance

Overlays are seen on a stream, often small, often over busy game footage.
Test your theme against a real background, not a flat color. If the text is
hard to read at quarter-size, it's not done.

## A good theme respects the user's content

A chat theme exists to show chat, not to decorate around it. A death counter
exists to show a number. The look should serve the information, not bury it.

## A good theme is original

A reskin of an accepted theme with one color changed is not a new theme.
Bring a point of view: a specific mood, a specific use case, a specific
aesthetic that isn't already in the catalog.

## What doesn't get in

- Anything AI-generated.
- Low-contrast text that's unreadable on stream.
- Effects that fight the content (animations that distract, chips that
  obscure messages).
- Near-duplicates of existing catalog entries.
- Anything that depends on undocumented internals to work.
